Pug Facts!

History: Pugs originated in China, dating back to the Han dynasty (B.C. 206 to A.D. 200). Some historians believe they are related to the Tibetan Mastiff. They were prized by the Emperors of China and lived in luxurious accommodations, sometimes even being guarded by soldiers.

 

This is a video of the world's most famous pug. Doug.

Facts

Living Conditions

Pugs can't tolerate high heat and humidity because of a short muzzle. When your Pug is outdoors, watch him carefully for signs of overheating. Pugs are definitely housedogs and should not be kept outdoors.

Grooming

Despite their short coats, Pugs shed a lot. Be ready to be cleaning a lot of hair off furniture, floor, and your clothing.

Community

Pug enthusiasts are a fun-loving bunch. They love Pug get-togethers, Pug parades, and dressing up their Pugs.

 

 

Companionship

Pugs need constant human companion. If you own a pug, expect him to follow you around in the house, sit in your lap, and want to sleep in bed with you.

Feeding

Pugs are greedy eaters and will overeat if given the chance. Since they gain weight easily, they can quickly become obese if food intake isn't monitored carefully.
